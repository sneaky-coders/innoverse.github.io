Rules & Regulations

Rules & Regulations

Guidelines For Teams


A typical team comprises of 3 members

Every student must have their college identity card as their identification proof

Intercollege grouping for a team is not allowed

Participants must wear their identity card given by the hackathon community in the college premises

Participants must stay within the college premises and should occupy the places that are assigned to them


HACKATHON THEME

Blockchain

Cyber Security

AI & ML

Teams must submit their project work on the above-mentioned theme only.

No plagiarism/copy-paste will be entertained, if found the teams will directly be disqualified from the hackathon


EVALUATION CRITERIA

Initial Review: After 3 hours into the hackathon, a team of judges will conduct a preliminary review of all participating teams. Teams will have to present their ideas, explain their problem statement, and present their progress till that point. Based on this review, marks will be allocated in each criteria mentioned.

Second Review: At the end of the 9 hours, the teams will have to present their complete solutions to a panel of judges. The teams will be evaluated based on their creativity, technicality, feasibility, and impact

Third Review: The solution will be submitted for review, where judges will suggest changes. All suggestions will be consolidated, and will assign marks to them. Top 5 teams will be selected for Final presentation

Final Review: The top 5 teams will be selected for the final presentation, where they will have the opportunity to present their solution in front of audience. The final presentation will be followed by a Q&A session, where the teams can answer questions from the judges and the audience.

TEAM SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

Hackathon Dates: The hackathon is scheduled to take place from 22nd March 2024 to 23rd March 2024, spanning a period of two days.

Registration Requirement: In order to participate in the hackathon, teams must first register for the Innoverse Hackathon 2024 through the official Hackathon website. Only registered teams will be eligible to submit their projects. Teams that fail to register will not be entertained and will not be able to participate.

Submission Process: Once the problem statements are released registered teams will be provided with a submission form link, through which they must submit their projects. The submission form will require teams to upload their presentation (PPT) and any other necessary files related to their project. It is mandatory for teams to submit their projects through this form.

Note:Problem statements will be released 24 hours prior to the event. And it is mandatory to finish the submission process within the 12 hours once the problem statements are released.

Team Leader: Each team must appoint a team leader, who will be responsible for registering the team and submitting the project on behalf of all team members. The team leader will serve as the primary point of contact for the organizers.

Submission Contents: Teams are required to submit a detailed PPT outlining their idea and project. The PPT should provide a clear and concise explanation of the project, including its purpose, functionality, and any innovative features or technologies used.

Format Requirements: All details submitted must adhere to the prescribed format provided by the organizers. Failure to comply with the format requirements may result in the rejection of the submission.

Hackathon Start: The hackathon will commence immediately after the introductory sessions, which will provide participants with an overview of the event, the rules, and the judging criteria.

Disqualification: Teams that fail to submit their projects by the specified deadline will be automatically disqualified from the hackathon. It is essential for teams to ensure that they submit their projects on time to avoid disqualification.

Originality: While teams are allowed to use previously implemented hackathons as a basis for their projects, extra points will be awarded for original and innovative projects. Teams are encouraged to think creatively and come up with unique ideas that showcase their skills and creativity.

GENERAL HACKATHON RULES

All work on a project should be done during the Hackathon. Your code repository must be initialized at the beginning of Hackathon itself.

Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.

Teams must stop hacking once the time is up. However, teams are allowed to debug and make small fixes to their programs after time is up.

Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers' discretion. Reasons might include but are not limited to breaking the Competition Rules, breaking the Code of Conduct, or other unsporting behaviour.

Hackathon Judging Criteria and presentation rules will be disclosed an hour before the Hackathon begins.

Valid Bank account details must be provided when asked for the prize money and reimbursements to get

processed. Regular processing time is applicable for the prize money and reimbursements to get processed.

In case of any discrepancies, the final take shall lie with the organizer.
